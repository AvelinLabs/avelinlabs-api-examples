# Customer Success Renewal Playbook

The customer success team manages enterprise advisory accounts with a quarterly executive cadence and a structured renewal health review. Customer Success Managers are expected to translate account evidence into renewal actions, not only maintain friendly relationships.

## Renewal risk signals

- Delayed executive sponsor meetings.
- Declining usage by core advisory users.
- Unresolved implementation blockers before value review.
- Missing success metrics for the next renewal committee.
- Stakeholder turnover without a documented relationship map.

## Stakeholder mapping

Each account should maintain a current map of executive sponsor, day-to-day owner, technical contact, finance/procurement contact, and internal renewal owner. Relationship gaps must be flagged early enough to support recovery planning.

## Expansion motion

Expansion should be tied to documented value, usage patterns, and customer-approved business outcomes. Customer Success Managers should know when to introduce expansion, when to defer, and how to explain the evidence behind the recommendation.

## Operating cadence

The expected cadence includes monthly health review, quarterly executive business review, renewal risk checkpoint, and handoff notes for sales or advisory leadership. Review quality is judged by traceable evidence and clear next actions.
