# Manufacturing Quality Role Framework

The Quality Systems Specialist supports a manufacturing quality program where traceable documentation and audit readiness are central operating requirements. The role coordinates evidence, owners, and due dates across quality, production, and training teams.

## CAPA coordination

The specialist tracks corrective and preventive actions from intake through closure. They confirm owner assignment, due dates, root-cause summary completeness, evidence attachment status, and review readiness. They do not approve final quality decisions without designated quality leadership.

## Document control

SOPs, work instructions, and controlled forms must follow version control, approval routing, and retirement rules. The role should identify missing approvals, outdated references, and uncontrolled copies before audit review.

## Audit readiness

Audit preparation includes evidence packets, training completion checks, CAPA status summaries, document history, and open-risk notes. Clear traceability matters because auditors and quality leaders need to understand why each item was included.

## Regulated training

Training evidence must map to role requirements and current SOP versions. The specialist should flag expired, missing, or mismatched training records and coordinate remediation with supervisors.
