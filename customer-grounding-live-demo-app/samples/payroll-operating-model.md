# HR Advisory Payroll Operating Model 2026

The payroll advisory team supports client payroll operations across California, Texas, Florida, and New York. Senior Payroll Specialists coordinate with HR advisors, client service managers, finance contacts, and client operations leads to keep employee data accurate and payroll exceptions visible before each processing window.

## Operating model

ADP Workforce Now is the primary payroll operating system for active client payroll runs. Senior Payroll Specialists must know how to review employee profiles, pay groups, earnings and deduction codes, tax setup, time-off feeds, payroll previews, post-payroll registers, and exception reports inside ADP Workforce Now.

Excel reconciliation workbooks are used for exception review, retro adjustments, deduction checks, benefit reconciliation, off-cycle payroll notes, and audit follow-up. Specialists reconcile payroll exceptions by comparing ADP Workforce Now payroll previews, HR change logs, benefit deduction files, manager approvals, and prior-cycle variance notes.

The payroll team maintains a compliance calendar for every client account. The calendar tracks payroll approval windows, state filing milestones, benefit deduction review dates, new-hire reporting expectations, wage notice timing, final-pay risk windows, and advisory review deadlines.

## Required skills

A Senior Payroll Specialist must demonstrate practical skill in multi-state payroll, ADP Workforce Now, payroll exception reconciliation, Excel analysis, HR advisory communication, compliance calendar discipline, issue documentation, and audit-ready handoff writing.

The role requires the ability to identify missing employee data, conflicting pay-change approvals, retroactive adjustment risks, state tax-location mismatches, benefit deduction anomalies, timecard approval gaps, and payroll calendar conflicts. The specialist should separate facts, assumptions, unresolved questions, and recommended escalation path in every review note.

## HR advisory support

Senior Payroll Specialists support HR advisory teams; they do not replace legal, tax, or executive decision makers. They prepare evidence-backed issue summaries so HR advisory leads can decide whether a payroll issue needs client approval, legal review, tax review, benefits review, or immediate payroll correction.

Advisory support includes explaining payroll exceptions in plain language, preparing client-ready notes, identifying which records support a recommendation, and flagging where the available evidence is incomplete or inconsistent.

## Escalation rules and review flags

Escalate to HR advisory leadership when a payroll exception affects final pay, wage-and-hour timing, state tax setup, benefit deductions, termination pay, leave status, executive compensation, garnishment handling, or a client policy conflict.

Review flags should be raised when ADP Workforce Now data conflicts with manager approval, when the compliance calendar shows a missed approval window, when a retro adjustment spans multiple states, when benefit deduction files do not match payroll preview totals, when employee status is unclear, or when the source evidence is not strong enough for a final recommendation.

Specialists do not make final legal determinations. They identify payroll compliance risk indicators, document the supporting evidence, and escalate unresolved questions to HR advisory leadership. Review notes should distinguish direct payroll ownership from advisory support responsibilities.

## Audit expectations

Each payroll cycle should leave a clear handoff trail: source of the issue, affected employee group, ADP Workforce Now status, reconciliation status, compliance calendar impact, open questions, owner, due date, and escalation decision.

Audit expectations require that every payroll exception summary cite the records reviewed, explain the reconciliation logic, identify the review flag if one exists, and show whether HR advisory leadership approved the final handling. Evidence quality matters because advisory leads rely on the notes to make final decisions.
